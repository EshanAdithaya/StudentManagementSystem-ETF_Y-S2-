Please Be aware of these things while creating Password

Use a Strong and Unique Password:

  • Create a password that is both strong and unique for each of your accounts.
  • Mix Characters: Include a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters to increase the complexity of your password.
  • Avoid Personal Information: Avoid using easily accessible personal information such as names, birthdays, or addresses in your passwords.
  • Longer Passwords are Better: Opt for longer passwords as they are generally more secure. Aim for a minimum of 12 characters.
  • Steer Clear of Dictionary Words: Avoid using common words, phrases, or dictionary words in your passwords. Instead, create combinations that are not easily guessable.
  • Use Passphrases: Consider using passphrases, which are longer combinations of words or phrases, for added security.
